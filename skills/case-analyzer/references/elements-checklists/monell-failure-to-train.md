# Monell Liability - Failure to Train Checklist

**Claim**: 42 U.S.C. § 1983 - Municipal Liability (Failure to Train)
**Standard**: *City of Canton v. Harris*, 489 U.S. 378 (1989); *Connick v. Thompson*, 563 U.S. 51 (2011)

---

## Elements Overview

To prevail on Monell failure-to-train claim, plaintiff must prove:

1. [ ] **Constitutional violation by municipal employee**
2. [ ] **Training deficiency existed**
3. [ ] **Deliberate indifference to constitutional rights**
4. [ ] **Causation** (deficiency caused violation)

## Element 3: Deliberate Indifference

### Legal Standard (*Connick v. Thompson*)

**Standard**: City's failure to train amounts to "deliberate indifference" to constitutional rights

**NOT Mere Negligence**: Must be more than simple failure; must approach conscious disregard

### Path to Prove Deliberate Indifference

#### Option A: Pattern of Similar Violations (*Canton*)

**Proof Requirements**:
1. **Multiple prior incidents** of same type of violation
2. **Notice to municipality** (policymakers knew or should have known)
3. **Failure to act** despite notice
4. **Obvious solution** existed

#### Option B: Single-Incident Liability (*Canton* Exception)

## Additional Considerations

### No Qualified Immunity for Municipalities

**Critical Advantage**: Municipality cannot assert qualified immunity (*Owen v. City of Independence*)

**Strategic Impact**: If Monell claim succeeds, municipality liable (no QI shield)

---

### Damages Limitations

**Compensatory Damages**: Municipality liable for full compensatory damages

**NO Punitive Damages**: Cannot recover punitives against municipality (*City of Newport v. Fact Concerts, Inc.*)

**Attorney's Fees**: Available under § 1988

---

## Strategic Notes

### Monell vs. Individual Liability

**Advantages of Pursuing Both**:
- Individual defendant: Liable if QI overcome
- Municipality: Liable if Monell proven (no QI defense)
- Increases settlement leverage

**Settlement Dynamics**:
- Municipalities often more willing to settle (avoid discovery of pattern)
- Individual defendants rely on municipality's indemnification
